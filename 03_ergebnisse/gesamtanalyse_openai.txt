### Comparative Analysis of Otto Anthes' Short Stories from "Lübeck du seltsam schöne Stadt"

---

#### Story 1: Herrn Walthers letzte Fahrt
1. **PLOT SUMMARY**: Herr Walther, an aging poet, arrives in Lübeck after years of wandering. The city presents itself as a vibrant hub of activity, attracting Walther’s attention yet evoking a sense of despair regarding his fading relevance in a world driven by commerce and wealth. His hopes rise when he meets a kind Domherr, but joy turns to sorrow upon learning of his niece's engagement, leading him to reflect on the harsh realities of life as a poet amidst the clamor of business.

2. **SPATIAL REPRESENTATION OF LÜBECK**: Lübeck is depicted as a city of contrasts, initially enchanting with its domes and churches, yet later feeling oppressive and silent. Specific locations mentioned include the Dom and the Trave River, highlighting the city's rich cultural heritage and economic vitality. The description of bustling docks teeming with commerce underscores a vibrant, yet overwhelming atmosphere for the weary poet.

3. **THEMES & MOTIFS**: Central themes include the conflict between art and commerce, the burdens of aging, and the notion of nostalgia for a past when poetry had more value. The motif of silence juxtaposed with the chaos of business illustrates the poet’s isolation in a world dominated by material desires.

---

#### Story 2: Der falsche Kaiser
1. **PLOT SUMMARY**: The story revolves around a mysterious old man who resembles the long-dead Kaiser Friedrich II, sparking curious gossip among Lübeck's townspeople. His presence unsettles local authorities, particularly the Bürgermeister, leading to a confrontation. The old man asserts that belief shapes reality, revealing the power of faith over tangible truth before disappearing into the light.

2. **SPATIAL REPRESENTATION OF LÜBECK**: Lübeck is portrayed through its important political center, particularly the Rathaus, where much of the story unfolds. The imagery captures the bustling atmosphere of the market and the quiet tension in the Bürgermeister’s office, contrasting communal curiosity with authoritative skepticism. The old man's path into the light suggests transcendence beyond the physical limits of the town.

3. **THEMES & MOTIFS**: Key themes encompass the illusion of authority, the nature of belief, and the fragility of social order. The motif of resemblance raises questions about identity and power, particularly in how public figures relate to their legacy in the eyes of the populace.

---

#### Story 3: Die Liebesbrücke
1. **PLOT SUMMARY**: The narrative follows Emperor Karl IV's visit to Lübeck, where he must navigate a marital dispute with his wife, the Kaiserin. Misunderstandings arise, leading to her refusal to meet him. Eventually, the playful dynamics of their relationship endear them to the local populace as they witness the couple's reconciliation over an ornate bridge built for their clandestine meetings.

2. **SPATIAL REPRESENTATION OF LÜBECK**: The city is vividly described through its majestic structures like the Mühlendamm and the Rathaus, symbolizing power and community. The bridge serves as a physical connection and emotional metaphor, bridging gaps between the couple and symbolizing unity amid misunderstandings.

3. **THEMES & MOTIFS**: Themes of love, misunderstanding, and reconciliation are prominent. The motif of bridges suggests connection and communication, while the public's fascination with the Kaiser's domestic life reflects a desire for intimacy with authority figures.

---

#### Story 4: Die nagende Maus
1. **PLOT SUMMARY**: A traveling craftsman in Lübeck learns about a historical tale regarding a rosebush that protected the city, believed to have died because of a mouse. The story hints at how trivial matters can lead to great consequences, as the city once fell to Danish control. The craftsman, inquiring about the meaning of symbols, reflects a belief in the power of tradition and myth.

2. **SPATIAL REPRESENTATION OF LÜBECK**: Lübeck is illustrated through its historical landmarks, particularly St. Marien Church, emphasizing its storied past and cultural essence. The narrative evokes a nostalgic yet playful atmosphere as the protagonist engages in conversations about folklore and symbolism, grounding the city's identity in historical anecdote.

3. **THEMES & MOTIFS**: Central themes include the power of myth and memory, and the impact of seemingly small events on larger historical narratives. The motif of decay associated with the rosebush and the mouse speaks to vulnerability and fragility within societal constructs.

---

### Conclusion
Otto Anthes’s collection presents a nuanced portrait of Lübeck through rich narratives exploring the interplay between personal experience and collective memory. The city's physical spaces evoke emotional landscapes that reflect broader societal themes, such as art's struggle against the tides of commerce and the everyday dynamism between authority and belief. Each story captures a different facet of Lübeck, enriching the reader's understanding of this "strange beautiful city."

Here's a structured comparative analysis of the first three short stories from Otto Anthes's collection 'Lübeck du seltsam schöne Stadt,' focusing on the plot, the spatial representation of Lübeck, and the identified themes and motifs.

### 1. Die Zehnte

**PLOT SUMMARY:**
Die Zehnte handelt von Gerd Hartog, einem Bildschnitzer in Lübeck, der in einer unglücklichen Ehe lebt. Besessen von der Kunst, zieht er sich zunehmend in seine Werkstatt zurück, während seine Frau Philippine ihn ständig mit ihrem Kummer und ihren Klagen erdrückt. Während eines Festes, zu dem er zur Gestaltung eines Spiels der zehn Jungfrauen eingeladen wird, erkennt Gerd seine wieder aufkeimenden Gefühle für die schöne Gred Eicke, eine der Schauspielerinnen. Zwischen ihnen entwickelt sich eine heimliche Anziehung, welche schließlich zu einem leidenschaftlichen Kuss in einem versteckten Raum führt. Gerd wird jedoch von seiner Angst vor der Konsequenz seiner Gefühle überwältigt und, von der Tragik ergriffen, ertrinkt er in der Trave.

**SPATIAL REPRESENTATION OF LÜBECK:**
Lübeck wird als eine Stadt voller kultureller und gesellschaftlicher Dynamik dargestellt. Die Glockengießerstraße, das Tanzhaus und das Alte Haus sind zentrale Schauplätze, die sowohl die Topographie der Stadt als auch ihre Atmosphäre reflektieren. Diese Orte sind lebendig und symbolisch für die Interaktionen von Kunst, Gesellschaft und persönlichem Drama. 

**THEMES & MOTIFS:**
Die zentralen Themen der Geschichte sind das Spannungsfeld zwischen Kunst und Alltag, die schwierige Rolle der Frau und das Streben nach persönlichem Glück. Der Konflikt zwischen Gerds künstlerischer Freiheit und der Enge seiner ehelichen Bindung ist ein zentrales Motiv. Auch der Themenkomplex der bedingten Liebe und die vergängliche Natur von Glück und Jugend spiegeln sich in der Erzählung wider.

---

### 2. Herr Hinrich Kastorp

**PLOT SUMMARY:**
Die Geschichte handelt vom Bürgermeister Hinrich Kastorp, der eine Sängerkapelle für die St. Marien Kirche stiften möchte, um die Bürger Lübecks mit einer harmonischen Musik zusammenzuführen. Während er mit dem Kantor Clemens von Ritzerau darüber spricht, wird er dann durch die politischen Geschehnisse in der Stadt abgelenkt, darunter der Besuch des Herzogs von Sachsen und sorgsame Vorbereitungen zu dessen Empfang. Nach einer Reihe von politischen und sozialen Herausforderungen initiiert Kastorp schließlich seine musikalische Vision, die am Ende zu einem großen Erfolg führt, während sein früherer Plan, eine Sängerkapelle zu gründen, dem Tod des Bürgermeisters den Anstoß gibt.

**SPATIAL REPRESENTATION OF LÜBECK:**
Die Stadt Lübeck ist hier als politisches Zentrum dargestellt, das von Verantwortung, Pflicht und gemeindlichem Zusammenhalt geprägt ist. Die St. Marien Kirche fungiert als musikalischer und kultureller Mittelpunkt der Stadt, während das Rathaus als Brutstätte politischer Entscheidungen fungiert. Diese Schauplätze verdeutlichen sowohl die gesellschaftlichen Hierarchien als auch den Puls des urbanen Lebens.

**THEMES & MOTIFS:**
Wichtige Themen sind die Verquickung von Kunst und Politik, die Identität der Stadt und die Rolle der Musik als fördernde Kraft für das Gemeinwohl. Kastorps Sehnsucht nach Musik als Ausdruck von Gemeinschaft und seine Fähigkeit, verschiedene Kräfte zusammenzuführen, spiegeln seine Persönlichkeit wider – sowohl als Politiker als auch als Mensch. Der Konflikt zwischen persönlicher Leidenschaft und allgemeiner Verantwortung verleiht der Erzählung zusätzliche Tiefe.

---

### 3. Zwei Gelübde

**PLOT SUMMARY:**
Der Stadthauptmann Hans Holm kehrt nach seiner Gefangenschaft aus Frankreich zurück und muss sich zwischen zwei Gelübden entscheiden: dem ersten Gelübde, das er seiner Kindheit als Geistlicher ablegte, und dem zweiten, das er einer französischen Magd ablegte, die ihm während seiner Gefangenschaft half. In der Festgesellschaft, in der er seinen Nachfolger Hans Stammel einlädt, muss eine Gruppe von Männern darüber urteilen, welches Gelübde fördernswert ist. Letztendlich wird die Magd von Stammel geheiratet, und Holm zieht als lediger Mann in den Krieg, während er in einem tragischen Schluss für seine Entscheidungen bezahlt.

**SPATIAL REPRESENTATION OF LÜBECK:**
Lübeck wird hier als Ort des politischen und militärischen Lebens dargestellt. Der Raum ist durch die Interaktionen zwischen politischen und militärischen Akteuren geprägt. Der Festsaal, in dem die Speisen eingenommen werden, ist ein symbolischer Ort, an dem Themen von Loyalität und Ehre diskutiert werden, was die kulturelle Identität der Stadt widerspiegelt.

**THEMES & MOTIFS:**
Die zentralen Themen sind Loyalität, Ehre, und die Dualität von menschlichen Begierden und religiösen Pflichten. Die rivalisierenden Gelübde des Hauptmanns verdeutlichen den inneren Konflikt zwischen Leidenschaft und Pflicht. Die Erzählung hebt den Einfluss des Schicksals hervor und beleuchtet, wie Entscheidungen im Leben weitreichende Folgen haben.

---

In diesen Geschichten schafft Anthes ein eindrucksvolles Bild von Lübeck, das sowohl die inneren Konflikte der Protagonisten als auch die äußeren gesellschaftlichen Strukturen erfasst. Der Spannungsbogen zwischen persönlichem Streben und kollektiven Erwartungen ist ein durchgehendes Element, das die komplexe Beziehung zwischen Individuum und Gesellschaft thematisiert.

### 1. Cordula Königin

**PLOT SUMMARY:**
In the autumn of 1519, a weary young man, Gustav Erichson, arrives at the Fuhrmannsherberge before Lübeck's Holstentor. He draws suspicion from the locals due to his foreign speech and appearance. As tensions rise, the famous Lübeck merchant Cord König intervenes, revealing his own connections to the political turmoil involving the young man’s escape from Denmark. Gustav find refuge in König's home, where he meets Cordula, Cord's daughter. Over time, a bond forms between Gustav and Cordula, exploring themes of duty, love, and power. Ultimately, Gustav prepares to return to Sweden as a leader.

**SPATIAL REPRESENTATION OF LÜBECK:**
Lübeck is depicted as a site of political intrigue and social tensions, with specific locations like the Holstentor and the marketplace. The atmosphere shifts from suspicion to camaraderie, symbolizing the protective nature of the city amidst external conflicts.

**THEMES & MOTIFS:**
Key themes include the juxtaposition of power and love, nationalism versus personal desire, and the role of commerce in shaping identities. The motif of waiting and anticipation in both personal relationships and political matters recurs throughout the narrative.

### 2. Der freche Freier

**PLOT SUMMARY:**
The story of Junker Heinz von Lüdersdorf unfolds when he enters Lübeck only to provoke a scandal by attempting to jump over the barricades set by the city council that block the way for pedestrians during significant meetings. Arrested for his audacity, he initially pleads for the hand of the Mayor's daughter, Anka, in an unconventional manner. Anka, intrigued but resolute, devises a plan to spring him from jail but ultimately respects the Junker's honor in keeping his word.

**SPATIAL REPRESENTATION OF LÜBECK:**
Lübeck is characterized in a lively manner, especially through the bustling Breite Straße and the intimate settings of the Rathaus and its surroundings, highlighting the city's mix of authority and social life. The atmosphere oscillates between festive excitement and the seriousness of civic duties.

**THEMES & MOTIFS:**
Themes of recklessness, honor, social norms, and the interplay between social status and personal desire are prominent. Additionally, the satirical view of nobility's folly versus the cunning intelligence of the common folk enriches the narrative.

### 3. Eulenspiegel

**PLOT SUMMARY:**
The iconic figure Till Eulenspiegel arrives in Lübeck, an embodiment of mischief and wit. His experiences reflect his subversive humor as he interacts with the townsfolk, attempting to establish his notoriety. His antics lead to critical encounters with city authorities, exposing the absurdity and hierarchies within Lübeck society, ultimately culminating in a humorous smackdown against bourgeois rigidity.

**SPATIAL REPRESENTATION OF LÜBECK:**
Lübeck is portrayed as a vibrant city in spring, filled with life as Eulenspiegel navigates between the Holstentor and the market square. The social environment is lively but also unforgiving, mirroring Eulenspiegel’s own tumultuous relationship with authority.

**THEMES & MOTIFS:**
Major themes include the critique of authority, the nature of humor as a social weapon, and a celebration of the underdog. The consistent motif of duality—between the serious and the comedic—highlights Eulenspiegel's role as a trickster.

### 4. Meister Markrabe

**PLOT SUMMARY:**
Meister Markrabe, a humane jailkeeper in Lübeck, distinguishes himself by treating criminals with kindness and educating them in music rather than punishing them harshly. His unconventional methods gain him respect, but he faces challenges with a repeat offender named Jochen Kluth, who pledges that everything belongs to everyone. The story tackles themes of compassion and redemption through art.

**SPATIAL REPRESENTATION OF LÜBECK:**
The setting places emphasis on the Marstall and the bustling market life of Lübeck. The environment is one of warmth and potential change, shaped through music that transforms both the characters and the community.

**THEMES & MOTIFS:**
Themes of compassion, community, and the redemptive quality of art permeate the story. The motif of music as a unifying force highlights the possibility of change for even the most hardened individuals.

---

This analysis captures the essence of each story within the collection, emphasizing the narrative arcs, spatial representations of Lübeck, and recurring themes and motifs that reflect larger societal and historical contexts. Further stories in this collection can be similarly analyzed to expand upon this comparative study.

### 1. Lübecker Marzipan
**PLOT SUMMARY:**  
Engelbrecht Hückefeld, a baker's apprentice in Lübeck, embarks on a journey to learn the best baking techniques to win the heart of Margret, the baker's daughter. After traveling and learning in various towns, he finds himself in Venice, where he learns to make marzipan from a renowned master. However, upon returning to Lübeck, his attempts to impress Margret with his new skills initially fail. After realizing the difference in taste preferences, he ultimately creates a Lübeck-style marzipan that delights her, leading to a romantic connection.

**SPATIAL REPRESENTATION OF LÜBECK:**  
Lübeck is portrayed as a place of tradition and craftsmanship, especially through the baker's shop where much of the story takes place. Specific locations invoked include the workshop and later a sense of homecoming. The contrast is emphasized with Venice, a foreign city, highlighting Lübeck's domestic familiarity against the exotic.

**THEMES & MOTIFS:**  
Central themes include the struggle for approval, the contrast between tradition and innovation, and romantic ambition. The story also explores notions of identity linked to one's craft and regional pride, as shown in the different styles of marzipan.

---

### 2. Die Geschiedenen
**PLOT SUMMARY:**  
A farmer from Wulfsdorf marries a beautiful woman from Vorrade, but their marriage quickly devolves into conflict. After a year, the wife leaves, and the husband remains unmovable in his solitude for twenty years. Eventually, pressed by desire for a young maid, he seeks divorce. However, the court proceedings lead him to reconsider, resulting in an unplanned reunion with his ex-wife, and they reconcile unexpectedly on their return home.

**SPATIAL REPRESENTATION OF LÜBECK:**  
Lübeck serves as a backdrop for legal matters, embodying the bureaucratic and judicial challenges in the farmer's life. The journey from Wulfsdorf to Lübeck, and ultimately the return home, signals a physical and emotional quest for resolution and understanding.

**THEMES & MOTIFS:**  
Themes of regret, isolation, and the complexities of love and marital commitments are central. The story examines societal norms and the personal consequences of marriage, and the balance of power within relationships, depicted through the farmer's evolving feelings toward his wife and maid.

---

### 3. Das Ochsenwunder
**PLOT SUMMARY:**  
In the early 19th century, Peter Schöppe, an artistically inclined boy, draws only lions or 'Löwen' instead of focusing on his studies. His father, a strict painter, tries to divert his passion to commerce. However, after a significant incident with an escaped ox in the streets of Lübeck, Peter finds inspiration that leads him to embrace his artistic abilities, resulting in a pivotal moment where he gains his father's acceptance for pursuing art.

**SPATIAL REPRESENTATION OF LÜBECK:**  
The story vividly describes Lübeck's streets, particularly the Fischstraße and the Breiten Straße, reflecting a city undergoing changes as Peter's artistic transformation unfolds against a backdrop of cultural and societal transition.

**THEMES & MOTIFS:**  
Themes of self-discovery, parental conflict, and the relationship between freedom and creativity dominate the narrative. Peter's story underscores the idea of overcoming societal expectations and pursuing one's passion despite obstacles.

---

### 4. Der tapfere Pappsnut
**PLOT SUMMARY:**  
The story follows Pappsnut, a nose-less innkeeper in Lübeck during the Napoleonic Wars, who cleverly protects his bar while also befriending the wealthy Madame Confurius next door. When French soldiers attempt to pillage her house, Pappsnut uses disguises and quick thinking to fend them off, ultimately earning Madame Confurius's gratitude and furthering the humorous depiction of his character.

**SPATIAL REPRESENTATION OF LÜBECK:**  
The Beckergrube and the vicinity of the harbor serve as rich locales that reflect both everyday life and historical tensions during wartime. The physicality of the tavern and its neighborhood reflects the community’s resilience and humor amid invasion.

**THEMES & MOTIFS:**  
Key themes include satire on class differences, bravery in the face of danger, and the humorous side of hardship. Tolerance and camaraderie are also motifs, particularly illustrated through Pappsnut's unyielding spirit to humor and defy his circumstances.

---

Overall, Anthes' stories weave together the fabric of Lübeck, exploring personal and social dynamics while showcasing the city's emotional landscape. Each narrative provides a distinct sense of place, set against timeless themes of love, ambition, and resilience.

### Comparative Analysis of "Spuk im Tünkenhagen"

#### 1. PLOT SUMMARY:
"Spuk im Tünkenhagen" recounts the tale of a baker's family living in Lübeck who encounters a series of supernatural disturbances attributed to a ghost. The family, comprised of the baker, his wife, and their teenage daughter, is initially disturbed by strange voices expressing complaints and accusations. The source of these disturbances is revealed to be Erna, a maid who possesses the unusual talent of ventriloquism, allowing her to mimic the ghostly voice. As the disturbances escalate, the family's reputation declines, drawing unwanted attention from curious townsfolk and eventually the police. Despite the chaos, Erna reveals that her only motivation was the enjoyment of her prank, leading to her eventual recruitment by an entertainment agency. The story culminates in a reflection on the dichotomy between artistic freedom and commercial exploitation.

#### 2. SPATIAL REPRESENTATION OF LÜBECK:
Lübeck is depicted through the intimate setting of the baker's home, particularly the rooms where the ghostly occurrences take place, such as the upstairs where Erna and the daughter sleep. The ambiance turns from mundane to eerie as the ghost’s voices disrupt the family's life, creating a sense of suspense and social disturbance in the community. Specific locations are only vaguely mentioned (Langen Lohberg, Tünkenhagen), emphasizing the story's focus on domestic space rather than grand city landmarks. This localized setting allows for a deep emotional connection, illustrating how communal fabric can be torn apart by supernatural intrigue.

#### 3. THEMES & MOTIFS:
Key themes include:
- **Nostalgia and Aberglaube (Superstition)**: The presence of an inexplicable ghost taps into the romanticized view of the past and the lingering belief in the supernatural within a modernizing society.
- **Art vs. Commerce**: Erna’s talent is initially met with awe but transforms into a product to be commercialized, highlighting the tension between artistic expression and societal expectations.
- **Community and Disruption**: The ghost's antics create a rift within the baker's family and disrupt the local community’s dynamics, showcasing how gossip and superstition can affect social cohesion.
- **Identity and Voice**: The duality of Erna’s existence as a maid and a ventriloquist underscores the complexities of identity, particularly regarding social class and creative potential.

This story encapsulates the interplay of personal aspirations and societal perceptions, set against the rich backdrop of Lübeck.
